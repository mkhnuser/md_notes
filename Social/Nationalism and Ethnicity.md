# Nationalism and Ethnicity

## The making of Identity

### Ethnicity

Here are the aspects which constitute ethnicity by Anthony Smith:

* A collective name:

      Armenians, Belarusians, etc.

* A believe in common ancestry:

      It has to be a believe, not a true fact.

* Shared historical memory;
* Association with a homeland;

      This homeland can be virtual, imagined.

* Sense of solidarity.

You differentiate ethnic groups by: color, language, physical appearance.

### Nation

Here are the aspects which constitute nation, by Anthony Smith:

* Land:

      A nation occupies concrete land,
      and this land is economically and culturally connected.

* Politics.
* Political tradition.
* Mass culture:

      Press, books, social media.

Roughly, a nation is formed when an ethnic group:

* Has a concrete territory;
* Has politics;
* Has culture.

Understand: state != an ethnic group.

### State

State is a group of people with a monopoly of force on a given territory, by Max Weber.

## Ethnic Identity

### An emergence of ethnic identity

An ethnic identity starts to emerge when:

* People amalgamate into large communities and cities.

      Therefore, they start speak almost the same language,
      not just separate dialects of a language.

* Conquest and assimilation happens.
* Kinship is formed.

## Origins of Nations

### The origins of nations and ethnic identities

Is an ethnic identity invented by a court of intellectuals,
or does it have a deeper, ancestral, cultural foundation?

Modernists believe in the former, whereas ethnosymbolism claims the latter.
Moreover, there is a view which is based on biology, which is called primordialism.

## Nation-building

### How do you build nations?

Anthony D. Smith describes the formation of nation
out of an ethnic group as the Triple Revolution:

* Politics:

      Bureaucracy.
      Mass conscription.

* Economic:

      The control of money and economic processes.
      Railroads allow for faster communication.

* Culture:

      A standardization of a language.
      Education system.
      Holidays, a national flag, a national anthem.

In France and other countries,
the case of a language is particularly interesting because of:

1. Social mobility a standardized language provides.
2. A prestige of a language.

## National Identity

Understand: nationalism can be civic and ethnic;
civic one relies on a state, whereas an ethnic one relies on culture & people.

## Everyday Nationalism

### Bottom up nationalism

Nationalism can be fostered by everyday activities and rituals
such as sports, consumption of goods, common places. In this sense,
nationalism can emerge from bottom up.

### Perception

People perceive national symbols differently.

In the case of a national flag, different groups might use it to achieve different objectives;
the meaning behind the usage of a Canadian flag during Canadian farmers protest is quite different from the meaning behind the usage of this flag by the Canadian government.
