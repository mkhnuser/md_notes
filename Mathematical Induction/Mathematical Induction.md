# Mathematical Induction

## Examples

### MMI and Generalization by Generic Particular

Understand: you can powerfully combine mathematical induction with
generalization by generic particular.

See Velleman's "How to Prove It", third edition, Section 6.1 exercises on this.

### Integer Parity Exclusiveness

It can be showed that every integer is either even or odd but not both.

1. First prove the statement for every natural number.
2. Then divide by cases for an arbitrary chosen integer.
3. Finally, generalize by generic particular.

See Velleman's "How to Prove It", third edition, Section 6.1 exercises on this.

## Strong Induction

One can use strong induction when a set of values is required in the
inductive step. Not just the previous value.

See Velleman's "How to Prove It", third edition, Section 6.4, Fibonacci Numbers discussion.
